# FanGeo Community Guidelines

Last updated: May 22, 2026

Draft for App Store submission review. This is not legal advice.

## Sports Rivalry Is Welcome

FanGeo is built for local sports fans. Cheer hard, debate teams, celebrate wins, and talk about the scoreboard. Keep it about sports and community.

## Abuse Is Not Allowed

Do not target people with harassment, threats, hate speech, slurs, bullying, stalking, sexual comments, or repeated unwanted contact.

## Not Allowed

- Harassment, bullying, threats, or targeted abuse.
- Hate speech, racism, slurs, or discrimination.
- Doxxing, sharing private information, or pressuring users to reveal personal details.
- Impersonating users, venues, teams, leagues, FanGeo, or public figures.
- Spam, scams, phishing, fake promotions, scraping, or repeated disruptive posts.
- Unsafe meetup behavior, coercion, stalking, or encouraging violence or illegal acts.
- Sexual exploitation, explicit content, or content involving minors.
- Misleading venue or business claims.

## Fan Chats and Comments

Keep Fan Chats and venue/event comments useful for the local sports crowd. Do not derail threads with spam, personal attacks, false venue information, or coordinated abuse. Report content that violates these rules.

## Direct Messages

Direct messages are for respectful conversation with people who accepted a friend connection. Do not send abusive, threatening, sexual, scam, spam, or repeated unwanted messages. If someone asks you to stop, stop.

## Meetups and Real-World Safety

Use good judgment before meeting anyone in person. Meet in public places, avoid sharing sensitive personal information, and leave situations that feel unsafe. FanGeo is not responsible for offline conduct between users.

## Report and Block

Use in-app report tools for abusive users, comments, messages, conversations, or venue issues where available. Block users who should not contact you. Reports should be truthful and made in good faith.

## Enforcement

FanGeo may warn users, remove content, hide comments, restrict messaging, suspend accounts, delete accounts, preserve reports, or take other action depending on severity, context, and repeat behavior.
